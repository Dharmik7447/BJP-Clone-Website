Bhartiya Janta Party [BJP]

(Naam to suna hi hoga)

The Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP), founded in 1980, is today India’s largest and most dominant political party. It emerged from the Bharatiya Jana Sangh and was re-established after the Janata Party experiment collapsed following the Emergency (1975–77). Strongly rooted in the ideology of cultural nationalism (Hindutva), integral humanism, and economic development, the BJP positions itself as the principal alternative to the Congress-led secular-liberal ecosystem. Under the leadership of Atal Bihari Vajpayee and L. K. Advani in the 1990s, it grew from just two Lok Sabha seats in 1984 to forming the central government in 1998–2004 as the leader of the National Democratic Alliance (NDA). The party’s symbol is the lotus, and its current ideological mentor remains the Rashtriya Swayamsevak Sangh (RSS).



Since 2014, under the leadership duo of Prime Minister Narendra Modi and party president Amit Shah (later J.P. Nadda), the BJP has achieved unprecedented electoral success, winning absolute majorities in the Lok Sabha in 2014 and 2019, and returning to power in 2024 as the largest partner in the NDA. It governs in 18 states either directly or in coalition (as of November 2025) and has expanded its footprint dramatically in eastern and southern India. Key policy initiatives under BJP rule include the abrogation of Article 370, the Citizenship Amendment Act, GST implementation, Ram Temple construction in Ayodhya, and ambitious welfare schemes such as Ayushman Bharat and PM-KISAN. With a membership of over 18 crore, the world’s largest political party continues to blend Hindutva, nationalism, and development (“Sabka Saath, Sabka Vikas, Sabka Vishwas, Sabka Prayas”) as its core political narrative.

Party History:

  • Founded as Bharatiya Jana Sangh on 21 October 1951 with Dr. Syama Prasad Mookerjee as its first President.
  • Re-established as Bharatiya Janata Party on 6 April 1980 with Shri Atal Bihari Vajpayee as founding President.

Ideology:

  • Rooted in national integration, opposing special status for Kashmir and supporting Goa liberation
  • Emphasizes cultural and nationalistic values through movements like Ayodhya and anti-corruption efforts.

Leadership's Key figures:

  • Dr. Syama Prasad Mookerjee (founder of Jana Sangh)
  • Shri Atal Bihari Vajpayee (founding President 1980-1986, multiple-term Prime Minister)
  • hri L.K. Advani (President 1986-1990 and 1993-1998, Deputy Prime Minister)
  • Shri Narendra Modi (Prime Minister since 2014, leading 2014, 2019, and 2024 elections)

Acheivements:

  • Debuted with 3 seats in 1952 elections
  • Peaked at 303 seats in 2019 Lok Sabha
  • Governs 21 states via NDA
  • Led Pokhran-II nuclear tests (1998) and Kargil War victory (1999)
  • secured first full majority in 2014 with 282 seats.

Party's Vision:

  • Committed to a strong, developed India via 'Ek Bharat, Shreshtha Bharat'
  • Focuses on societal transformation for all under PM Shri Narendra Modi's leadership.
